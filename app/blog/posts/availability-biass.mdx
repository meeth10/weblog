I wanted to go on a trip, I wanted to visit the mountains and go trekking and was shot down at once by my mother. She said every year thousands die by falling of the cliff. I couldn’t argue much but I did draw a very important conclusion that day. We severly overestimate the chances of something small happening to us and significantly underestimate the chances of something big happening to us.

Take the example of smoking; We know in the long run it is harmful but we underestimate and down play the effects it has on us. While wining the lottery is a 1 in million chance yet people line up to get rich quick. The point remains we are so influenced by information available to us, that we cannot take a new bias into consideration.

Availability bias works in a very weird way. You would rather choose to consume wrong information over no information. You would take any new information to re confirm your bias it happens with us and Ai.

With Ai it’s even more strong, at the start you provide a bias to preference X and then try to provide more information against X. The Ai would find ways to prove why preference X is still better. ( sorry for so many x’s)

My point here is more you hang out with people with varied view less likely you are to fall to this bias. It is hard to detach from a said preference but if you don’t want to fall a victim to this bias, you need to move away from “opinions”.
