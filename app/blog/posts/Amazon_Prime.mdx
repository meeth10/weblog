Introduction -

Amazon Prime Videos or popularly known as Prime Videos, is a vidoe streaming platform, which charges monthly / yearly subscription and occasionally getnerates ad revenue. An estimate of 250 Million users use/subscribed to prime videos worldwide. Prime Videos have 3 main categories of Income - Subscription Model, Ads, Rent/ Buy Movies. Out of Amazon's 638 B the subscriptions account for 44B in revenue. Not all can be through Prime Videos, In 23 there was around 14 B accounted as Primes Revenue assuming a steady growth of 8% we would have around 16B dollars as of 2025. Despite this large numbers Prime Videos is not making profits, In 2024 an Estimate of 21B was spent for content generation. Despite having large presence globally Primes is not a profit making business as of 2024.

Analysis -

Porters 5 Forces:-
a) Threat of New Entrant -LOW/MODERATE - Capital Intensive Business as a lot of cash needs to be thrown to create new and engaging content. You also need a global distribution infrastructure. While this is true for global markets, a regional market would be easier to break through as it caters to a niche market.

b) Bargaining Power of Buyer - HIGH - Indian Markets are very price sensitive while this might not be true in American markets. Even removing the price point the users have gotten so used to good content they switch very quickly and cancel subscriptions. The presence of multiple options doesnt help either.

c) Bargaining Power of Supplier - HIGH - Bollywood / Hollywood stars require large amounts to join your projects. Disney, Warner Brothers take high licensing fees. Local and global markets both can leverage content produced and demand high prices.

d) Threat of Substitiue - HIGH - Without considering regional threat which pose a high threat too, you have cable tv, you have music and sports and various forms of entertainment and with attention span decreasing reels and short videos are potential substitutes to OTT.

e) Competitive Rivarly - HIGH - Removing airlines this is the most competitive market where you have Netflix, Disney, HBO, Apple, Regional OTTS and YouTube. With giants like this competing to acquire new customers with original content it would be safe to call this space fierce.

Key Competitors -

Direct Competiton -
1. Netflix - The bigget giant of them all with around 300M subscribers producing a revenue of around 40B is Power house. It Spends around 17B on content creation alone.
2. Disney - While Disney might not have as many subscribers as Netflix does but produces revenue from other streams more imprortanly IP. In 2019 around 66% of Disney’s revenue came because of IP rights.
3. HBO Max - Another IP giant relies on a mixture of content across HBO, Max Discovery and Warner Brothers to provide its value generating nearly 10B in Revenue.

Indireact Competions -
1. YouTube - Prime and other OTT platforms provide content as a distraction or to relax and those are not the only ways to do so YouTube for ages have been providing content and that too with inclusion of shorts more people switch to YouTube for a short period of relaxation instead of OTT.
2. Instabram and TikTok - these are more messaging apps than content creation but with advance of reels people prefer to doom scroll rather than watch something meaning full thus removing future dependency of OTT platform
3. Gaming, Sports and other content - People are going back to watching sports, esports and other content(soap opera) to pass time and rarely tune to wach new shows.

Disruptor - Possible Ai generated content could be the nex wave of entertainment which could wipe out OTT platforms like they did to DVD and CD stores.

Financial Analysis :
For 2023 - Estimate Revenue is 12B. With it growing 12% a year and a CAGR of 10-12% over 5 years just below netflixs 14%. But AD revenue has increased significantly growing from 440 M in 2024 to an estimated 800M 2025. In 2023 Prime was in losses as content creation led to an Esimate of 18B, This is not a significant contributor to primes revenue but does help inPrimes retention. There's also a very High Fixed Cost as you have to pay for the content you buy or make but low variable cost. Primes Videos standalone is a bad business but bundling with primes helps retain customers and Primes videos has led to a new source of income in the form of ad revenue. Treating it as a standalone business and seeing those disatours numbers is not right.

SWOT
Strength -
1) Amazon Ecosystem - Prime Videos sits well in the whole amazon ecosystem of prime music, alexa, fire tv.
2) Financial Backing - Have the money to acquire ip rights or spend on content creation
3) Bundling Power - Prime Users are likely to watch and use Prime Videos.

Weakness -
1) Lacks IP - Disney and HBO own a lot of the content that's used and if they are not making money from their subscriptions they definitely are making money via IP.
2) User Interface - Arguably the worst user interface out there.
3) Low Engagement - Treated more as an Add on service rather than a stand alone service, lowest user time spent across platforms.

Opportunities -
1) Sports streaming dominance - They could exclusively target sports and create it as a Moat.
2) Regional Content - Soap operas and more localsied shows to engage with audiences.
3) Ad revenue - Using the above and generating income using Ads would be the logical move.
