I have been an avid reader and part-time investor for lil over half a decade. And in all my little experience I have learnt few things one of which being that some data is more important than others!

Even almighty Ai is just as good as the date it has. I was told that in life and data analytics both it’s more important to know what you don’t want, when compared to what you want!

Let me deviate for a moment and go back to my roots in electronics and a subject i absolutely loved CCN; Where we described the rate of data transfer(Throughput) is same as slowest data transfer of any subsystem. Basically even the most complicated systems fall back to the critical parameters and everything else is insignificant ( less significant).

In businesses you have a similar thing going on when you work on consulting cases and you strip down profitability into Revenue and Cost and so on. This is done till you find the critical point. An Ideal KPI board would have 4 parameters that could break or define businesses. I worked as intern for a consulting firm where I was making their KPI board and was furious at having 10-15 parameters to find various issues in the business.

I would suggest borrowing something from Engineers while making you KPI dashboard; We( I am an Engineer btw) call it partial differentiation. You keep others constant and focus on one parameter only and see how it impacts the output in focus.

“It is better to have an approximate answer to the right question than an exact answer to the wrong question.”

John Tukey, statistician ( expected it to come from a statistician only).
