# 🎯 TAM, SAM, SOM: Cutting Through the Jargon  

Every ambitious founder, investor, or strategist eventually faces the same blunt question: *“How big is the market?”* The answer isn’t a single number—it’s a layered story told through **TAM, SAM, and SOM.**

- **TAM (Total Addressable Market):** The universe. If every potential customer on the planet bought your product, this is the revenue ceiling.  
- **SAM (Serviceable Available Market):** The part of the universe you can actually reach given geography, regulation, and business model.  
- **SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market):** The slice you’re realistically going to capture in the next few years. Think of it as your plate at the buffet.  

---

## Why Should Anyone Care?  
Because strategy without sizing is just wishful thinking. TAM, SAM, SOM puts discipline into ambition. It answers:  

- Are you chasing a **trillion-dollar dream** or a **$200M niche**?  
- Are you frying a **tuna** or a **sardine**?  

Investors want to know the upside, founders need to know the runway, and consultants use it to filter which opportunities are even worth the PowerPoint slides.  

---

## Why Visualize It?  
Numbers alone don’t persuade. “A $10B market with a $500M obtainable slice” sounds dry. Visualized, however, the concentric circles of TAM → SAM → SOM make the story instantly clear.  

It’s far simpler to *see* the narrowing funnel than to listen to someone over-explain it. That’s exactly why we built the [TAM Simulator](https://tam-simulator-awpvrysww9puljrkcvnypo.streamlit.app/)—to make sizing intuitive.  

---

## A Quick Reality Check  
Most companies overestimate TAM and underestimate execution challenges in SOM. Visualization forces humility: you can’t claim to fry a whale when the plate clearly shows a sardine.  

The exercise doesn’t just answer “how big is the fish”—it sparks the right follow-on questions:  
- Is this market growing or stagnant?  
- What barriers stop me from expanding SAM into TAM?  
- What edge allows me to grab more of SOM than my competitors?  

---

TAM, SAM, SOM is not an academic ritual. It’s the **first sanity check** for any strategy.  

Done right, it shows whether you’re playing in the kiddie pool or the open ocean. Done poorly, it convinces no one.
